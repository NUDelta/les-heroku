Installing and Using LES

Thank you for telling us about your notification preferences! Your account is now created and fully setup. Please follow the instructions below to install LES on your phone. These instructions will also be on your home page whenever you log back into this website.

Installation Instructions

If you have previously participated in a Delta Lab study for LES, delete any old version of LES before installing. To install LES, launch Safari on your iPhone and go to https://i.diawi.com/. Alternatively, you can go to http://www.yellkey.com/, but this link will expire in 10 minutes. See image 1 for how to install.

After installation, attempt to open LES; you will likely need to trust the Enterprise Developer (image 2). To fix this, go to Settings -> General -> Device Management. On this screen, you will see image 3. Click on Northwestern University and then click Trust "Northwestern University" (image 4). After this, you will be able to run the application.

Once you launch LES, you will be asked for permission to track your location when you are near a place we are monitoring (ex. Coffee Lab, Free Food Event, etc) You must select "Always Allow" or your application will not work (image 4).

On the next screen, enter your account credentials (image 5). Your credentials will be the same Email Address and Password you used to create your account with.

Important Information

DO NOT explicitly terminate the application (swipe up on app)

This will prevent you from receiving any notifications when you pass locations. The application will automatically go into a low-power background mode when it is not open on your phone.

Please avoid using Low-Battery mode until completion of the study.

Low-battery mode interferes with our location tracking and prevents us from sending you notifications. If you must until you get to a charger, no worries at all but we will be unable to notify you about free food (there will be LOTS on both north and south campus over the next two weeks).

Tap on the notification to reopen LES if it detects LES has closed on your phone.

After using Low-Battery mode, you may need to reopen LES via this notification to have it work again.

Receiving and Responding to Notifications

There are generally 3 ways to respond to notifications that LES sends you.

Swiping Left: iPhone 6/6+ and iPhone SE

When notified, swipe the notification to the left and click view (see image 2). As a result, you will see multiple options that can be selected as a response to the notification (image 3). Please choose the one that best answers the question.

3D Touch: iPhone 6S/6S+ and above

When notified, press deeply info the notification (see image 3). As a result, you will see multiple options that can be selected as a response to the notification. Please choose the one that best answers the question.

Swiping Right: All iPhones

You can also tap or swipe right on the notification to respond to notifications (see image 4). We recommend the above interactions instead since they are much faster and you don't need to open your app to do it.

Daily Usage

General Usage Instructions

Please use the application in your daily life. You may be notified as you pass places, like Coffee Lab, or as you go inside places, like SPAC, to contribute more information about the location. Additionally, you may also be notified about going to locations that may not be nearby.

Go only if you are interested and able to at that time. Your participation is completely voluntary (in fact, we want to know why you do and don't go!) and will not affect your compensation as long as you complete the post-study survey.

Getting Information You Care About

A big part of LES is getting information about places and things you care about (ex. free food, seating at coffee shops). LES will notify you about places that have community-sourced information you care about (based on your preferences) when you are somewhat nearby. You can also view this information on the For You page (see below).
